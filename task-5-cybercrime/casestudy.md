# Task 5 – Cybercrime Case Study

## Topic: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is one of the most common cybercrimes in India, where attackers trick users into transferring money or revealing sensitive information such as OTPs or UPI PINs.

This fraud typically occurs in a step-by-step manner. First, the attacker contacts the victim through a phone call, SMS, or social media message, pretending to be from a bank, customer care service, or a trusted platform. Next, they create urgency by claiming issues like account suspension, refund processing, or prize winnings. The victim is then asked to click on a malicious link, scan a QR code, or enter their UPI PIN/OTP. Once the victim performs these actions, the attacker gains access and immediately transfers money from the victim’s account.

College students and young adults are frequent targets because they actively use UPI apps and may not always verify such requests. The consequences include instant financial loss, panic, and difficulty in recovering the money if not reported quickly.

Victims are advised to immediately report such incidents on the National Cyber Crime Portal (https://cybercrime.gov.in) or call the helpline number 1930. This case highlights the importance of awareness, cautious behavior, and secure digital practices while using online payment systems.
